Environmental

 

Environmental applications of Specto Shimatsu LIBS analyzers include direct inspection of contamination in soil, rocks, sands, sediments, rivers, and air.

LIBS scanners can also be used to monitor industrial pollutants such as electronic waste, landfill leachates, lubricating oils, and aerosols. Chemometrics, calibration, and machine learning are applied to LIBS data for material identification, discrimination, and classification.

Soil: elemental analysis (Al, Ca, Fe, Cu, …), soil classification (pH, texture, carbon, nutrients, toxics), spatial elemental mapping, and humification degree. Detection of soil/rock pollution by Ti, Cr, As, Ag, Mo, Pb with LOD < 10 ppm is achievable.

Rocks & sediments: in-situ discrimination and classification via geochemical imaging and mineral fingerprinting; analysis of Ba, Zn, Cr, Cu, Pb traces.

Waters & leachates: monitoring of trace elements and contaminants; key factors include P, N, C, heavy metals, COD, NOx, and SOx.

Air & exhaust gas: aerosol elemental analysis for air quality and source discrimination; exhaust monitoring (e.g., air-to-fuel ratio, CO₂). Targets include suspended heavy metals (e.g., Co, Ni) and combustion byproducts.

Waste & recycling: sorting and categorizing plastics, glass, and organics (e.g., LDPE, HDPE, PS, PVC, PET, PC). LIBS is also used for e-waste to identify and quantify metals in electronic components.

 

Ref: De Morais et al., Science of the Total Environment 837 (2022): 155699.  |  Stefas et al., Molecules 26.16 (2021): 4981.
