    Analysis

    Positioning & Use Case: Desktop LIBS Analyzer targets industrial labs that need high‑throughput elemental analysis with strong sensitivity to light elements (C, S, P) where conventional XRF struggles. It fits metallurgy (steel/alloy melt control), mining geochemistry, and R&D workflows that demand < 5 s decisions.

    Performance Drivers: The extended 177–410 nm UV range and 0.03–0.07 nm resolution improve line separation and detection at short wavelengths, enhancing accuracy for light elements. Adjustable 1–200 mJ pulse energy allows tuning ablation depth and SNR across matrices.

    Operational Reliability: Dual cooling (air + water) and an enclosed, rugged chassis are designed for continuous operation in lab environments (20–30 °C, < 60% humidity). Built‑in touchscreen reduces external PC dependencies and simplifies SOPs/training.

    Cost of Ownership: Using standard 99.99% industrial argon reduces gas costs versus high‑purity requirements. Short analysis time and minimal prep increase sample throughput and reduce per‑sample cost.

    Comparison to XRF: While XRF is strong for mid‑to‑heavy elements, LIBS excels in light‑element detection (C/S/P) and can better handle complex, inhomogeneous matrices with surface‑sensitive ablation.Desktop LIBS Analyzer complements or replaces XRF where light elements are critical.

    Compliance & Adaptability: Configurable methods allow tailoring detection limits and timing by matrix (ores, slags, pellets, steels). Optional methods can quantify total/metallic iron in iron‑based raw materials for process control.

    Summary

    Combining speed, accuracy, and adaptability, the Desktop LIBS Analyzer is a complete laboratory solution for uncompromised elemental analysis. It reduces operational costs while delivering laboratory‑grade precision, setting a new benchmark for UV‑LIBS performance.
