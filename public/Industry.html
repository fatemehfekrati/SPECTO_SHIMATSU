Industry

There are multiple industry challenges that could be addressed by Specto Shimatsu instrumentations. LIBS utilities in different industries enable quality control and process optimization. Emerging applications in food industries, waste management, solar and nuclear energy sectors are rising regarding the technology upgrades. Minimum or no sample preparation during online/ inline measurements make the LIBS as a common technique for industrial demands. The method is user friendly and minimally destructive for materials inspection.

Adaptable to be exploited in hostile environments Specto Shimatsu analyzers could discriminate the forensic cases, materials of a nuclear power plant, cracks in high-temperature chimney walls, airborne solid particles in the severe condition of a steel industry and elemental composition of the pipelines. Also, these detectors could characterize dangerous particulates in the high temperature molten metals. Remote characterization of different materials and contaminantsâ€™ detection could be accessible with LIBS systems too. Rapid and in-situ classification of different waste materials, plastics or metallographic scraps and laser ablation molecular isotopic spectrometry (LAMIS) of uranium isotopes are such examples. This makes the technique adaptable for being used in atmospheric researches or even space explorations.

One of the main applications of Specto Shimatsu systems in industry is quality control of products on the conveyer belt to complete the monitoring/ assessment procedures. The technique is applicable for quality control in solar cell industry to evaluate the titanium dioxide coating, mapping of carbon impurities in silicon or determining the concentration of sodium in CIGS cells. In food industry LIBS analyzers are served to detect micro-nutrient, contamination and adulteration of foods. There are several examples for applications of Specto Shimatsu detectors in agricultural industry such as rapid identification of the geographical origin of vegetable samples, evaluating the nitrogen and protein concentrations in wheat or classification of fruits considering their sugar or acidity percentage.

Quantitative analysis by LIBS is dependent on generation of calibration curves for different elements/base materials by certified reference materials (CRM)s via regression procedures, chemometrics and deep learning techniques. Employment of more CRMs could elevate the precision of LIBS sensing. Other decisive variant that effects the sensitivity of measurement is the quantified value for the number of photons scattered from each atom through LIBS emission. This variable is formulated by element-specific emission rate (EMRA) dependent on atomic transition and plasma temperature.

Normally the limits of detection (LOD) and limits of of quantification (LOQ) of multi elemental detection are typically in the ppm and ppb ranges which limits are desirable for the industry. Electronic transistors and board components of the gadgets of today life including cell phones, laptops, TVs, etc. are composed of more than 60 elements. Specto Shimatsu multi elemental analysis is capable of identification of complex medium simultaneously. In case of usage, all models of the handheld, stationary and scanning analyzers could be introduced to the industrial sectors. Regarding the practicality, green technique of LIBS has a crucial role in enhancing efficiency, quality control, in-situ analysis and sustainability.
