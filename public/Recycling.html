Recycling

Regarding the dominant potential of LIBS systems in sorting the materials, Spectoshimatsu analyzers are efficient in waste managements. Traditional mounted magnets or eddy current systems are able to separate ferrous and non-ferrous metals with strong magnetic properties or electrical conductivity respectively but incapable to distinguish between other metal alloys. Rapid automated LIBS could play the complementary role for traditional waste sorting units as the elemental compositional analyzer. Without the need for sample preparation, wide range of waste types could be targeted through multi-elemental nature of the technique. Waste of electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE), ELV (End of Life Vehicle) wastes, polymers such as polyoxymethylene, PVC, polytetrafluoroethylene, polyoxyethylene, PA, PE ,PP, ABS, HIPS,…. or even waste rocks and aggregates are distinguishable with LIBS. Monitoring and analyzing of low-cost incoming waste result in improvement of the value of the output product in recycling procedure.

E-waste as the fastest-growing waste stream globally with notable supplements of valuable metals of Au, Ag, Pt, Cu, Pd… LIBS analyzers are served for rapid classification of e-wastes such as printed circuit boards (PCBs) of mobile phones for efficient recycling of them. Besides, real-time monitoring of the elemental composition of the E-wastes during the process of valorous metal recovery is accomplished by LIBS systems. Based on spectral lines of oxygen, carbon and hydrogen, plastic content in waste of electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE) could be identified.

Spectoshimatzy analyzers are helpful in characterization of different plastic types relying on key spectroscopic line detections such as Bromine (Br) in flame retardant plastics, Chlorine (Cl) in chlorinated plastics such as PVC and CPE, Silicone (Si) in carbon fiber reinforced polymer (CFRP) materials or Fluorine (F) in PTFE (Teflon). The method is capable of classifying colored or black post-consumer plastics (PET, PE, PP and PS) based on their elemental composition regardless of their colors. Besides, identification of the dirty post-consumer recycled (PCR) plastics are usually challenging. The LIBS technique can overcome the problem by multi shot measurements through which the preliminary laser ablations.
