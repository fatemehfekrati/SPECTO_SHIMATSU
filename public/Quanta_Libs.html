Quanta LIBS

The Quanta LIBS system is a compact laboratory with minimal maintenance requirements that is functional in diverse industrial sites, harsh environments and landscapes.

Multi-purpose handheld LIBS analyzer facilitates real-time, precise and accurate field analyses. Comprehensive spectral dataset of reference library including index elemental emissions associated with optimized calibration methods bring about a reliable quantitative output by Quanta LIBS.

    • Product Detail

    This portable compact LIBS analyzer is a user-friendly instrument that requires minimum or no sample preparation and can be used in:

    • Inventory surveys
    • Alloy characterizations
    • QC/QA inspections
    • Positive material identification (PMI) and scrap recycling

    The spectral window around 1.6 μm laser wavelength is approximately 8000 times more eye-safe than many other laser irradiations.

    This method avoids hazardous X-radiation and also enables trace detection of light elements such as C and Li in comparison with expensive XRF testing.

    Quanta LIBS provides adaptable detection limits to support accurate grade identification. Verified limits of detection (LOD) for many elements such as Fe, Zn, Cu, Mn, Cr, Sn, Ti, Zr, Ni and others are below 10 ppm.

    Core benefits

    • Real-time gold grade estimation directly in the field
    • Smaller laser spot size for improved spatial resolution
    • Non-destructive purity assessment of precious and base metals

    Applications

    • Environmental monitoring: Detection of contaminations and hazardous elements in landscapes
    • Mining: Efficient and rapid sorting of raw materials and ores
    • Metal scraps recycling: Grade identification of metal alloys for pre-recycling classification
    • Academic research: Elemental detection in diverse fields such as archaeology, refinery investigations and medical research

    Critical measurements with this portable device extend from characterization of carbon and oxygen in steel to lithium oxide in lithium ores.

    Multi-shot measurements provide more accurate results by accounting for non-homogeneity in samples.

    The technique is effectively non-destructive due to the negligible laser-ablated area from individual pulses. The unmatched performance of this low-weight, durable system makes it a promising and cost-effective solution compared with traditional competitors.
