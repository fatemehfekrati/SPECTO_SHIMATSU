Coal

Coal remains a major source for global electricity generation and has been a frequent subject of LIBS research thanks to stand-off capability and broad elemental coverage. The elemental composition (major elements C, H, O, N, S) strongly impacts combustion efficiency and emissions.

Specto Shimatsu LIBS analyzers support multiple stages of coal handling and power production: coal quality control, combustion optimization, and fly/bottom ash analysis. Rapid inline or at-line measurements help minimize environmental impact while maximizing power output.


Compared to radiation-based analyzers, LIBS offers a non-nuclear approach without radiation safety concerns, while delivering multi-element detection and fast response suitable for process control.

In coal-fired plants, inline LIBS enables monitoring of ash content, sulfur (for SO2 control), and other key elements. Tracking unburned carbon in fly ash, heating value proxies, and moisture trends supports improved boiler efficiency. Specto Shimatsu analyzers are designed for integration near boiler control systems for real-time feedback.

References: Sheta, S. et al., Scientific Reports 11 (2021) 15968. — Pedarnig, J. D. et al., Applied Sciences 11 (2021) 9274.

Coal’s industrial index depends on elemental/chemical properties (e.g., volatile matter, fixed carbon, calorific value). Fast discrimination and classification of coal types supports cleaner operation and optimized energy yield.

Specto Shimatsu LIBS scanners provide user-friendly operation, minimal sample prep, and wide multi-element coverage. Typical targets in coal/ash include C, Mg, Al, Si, Ca, Fe, and Ti, measured inline with acceptable accuracy for process QA/QC.
