Biomedical

 

The widespread and growing applications of LIBS in biomedical applications are really outstanding. Different SpectoShimatsu systems such as scanners, benchtop models or even customized models could be appropriate for biomedical usages.

 

At early stages LIBS was introduced to medicine for elemental analysis during histopathological studies of pigments or vacuoles. This technique is capable of detecting exogenous contaminations and micro/nano particles. To avoid sophisticated challenges of biopsy, LIBS imaging conjugated with PCA data processing method provide the appropriate tool for cancer diagnosis over skin or other organs. . The analyses are based on direct imaging of different tissue types or blood/serum samplings. This capability facilities the surgeon with optimal tumor resection through distinguishing the healthy tissues from malignant textures based on the accumulation of elements such as Ca, K, Na or trace metals such as Mg and Cu in cancerous cells.

 

SpectoShimatsu systems are also capable of detecting the heavy metals, characterizing the cholesterol content and calcification in arteries, blood plasma and bones as indicator of anomalies over entire-organ scale. Accordingly, the method is adaptable with characterization of different types of cancer such as lymphoma, melanoma, glioma, nasopharyngeal carcinoma and liver/breast/cervix/lung/bone cancers. Evaluation of treatment efficacy or discrimination of the disease stage are accessible with LIBS analysis.

 

 

Ref: Busser, Benoit, et al. "Elemental imaging using laser-induced breakdown spectroscopy: A new and promising approach for biological and medical applications." Coordination Chemistry Reviews 358 (2018): 70-79.

 

LIBS can directly detect metal (Si, Gd, Au,etc.) NPs without requiring any chemical modification or labeling with dyes. This provides the technique with the ability of in-vivo tracing of the NP based drugs to study their degradation/stability/residence time. The efficient drug delivery could be screened by 2D LIBS imaging of NPs when targeting the malignant tumor cells by attributable medications.
