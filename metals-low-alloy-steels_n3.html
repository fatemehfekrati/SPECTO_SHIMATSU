Metals – Low Alloy Steels

Overview

Low alloy steels are widely used for pipes, automotive and aerospace bodies, railway lines, and offshore/onshore structural plates. High-strength low alloy (HSLA) grades are favored for corrosion resistance and strength-to-weight benefits. Low alloy steels typically contain <10% total alloying elements (C, Mn, Cr, Ni, Mo, V, Si). Accurate, fast elemental analysis is essential throughout steel production.

Handheld LIBS for Low Alloy Steels

The Low Alloy Steel (LAS) program provides a complete methodology—from surface prep to QA—for elemental analysis using handheld LIBS. Coverage includes HSLA, heat-resistant Cr-Mo steels, low-temperature Ni steels, weathering steels, and high-tensile steels, as well as cast iron, stainless steel, and high-Mn steels. The method is built on a master calibration derived from hundreds of certified reference materials (CRMs) to ensure precision and traceability.
