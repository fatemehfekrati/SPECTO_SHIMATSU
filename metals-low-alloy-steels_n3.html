Metals- Low Alloy Steels

Jan 17 , 2022

Metals: low alloy steels

Low alloy steels are widely used to produce pipes, automotive and aerospace bodies, railway lines and offshore and onshore structural engineering plates. High-strength low alloy steel is used due to its corrosion resistance properties. Low alloy steels typically contain less than 10% alloying elements (C, Mn, Cr, Ni, Mo, V and Si combined). Accurate and fast elemental analysis is a critical requirement during the production of steel.

 

Handheld LIBS analysis of low alloy steels

The Low Alloy Steel (LAS) program delivers a complete methodology from sample preparation to quality assurance for elemental analysis using Handheld LIBS and covers high-strength low alloys, high heat resistance steels (chromium-molybdenum steel), low temperature use steels, (nickel steels), weathering steels and high tensile strength steels. Cast iron. Stainless steel. High Mn Steel and all kind of steel. The method has been developed using a master calibration of more than hundreds certified reference materials.
